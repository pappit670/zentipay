01 — Pay anyone

Sending money
should feel easy.

Where it came from

Right now, paying someone means juggling till numbers, paybills and account numbers — then squinting to check you typed every digit right. Get one wrong and your hela is gone to a complete stranger. We watched people do this with their hearts in their mouths, and we thought: this is way too tense for something you do every day.

How it works

So we made it three moves. Type the amount, pick the person, swipe. Behind the scenes, Zenti quietly chooses the best way to send it — M-Pesa, your bank, or your Zenti balance — and tells you the second it lands. You never copy a number between apps again.

✓ It clears in seconds, to the right person, every single time. Hakuna stress.

02 — The Zen Tag

Your tag is
the new number.

Where it came from

A phone number is a fragile thing to hang your whole identity on. It’s tied to a SIM. It changes when you switch lines, it gets lost, it gets swapped. And reading out digits one by one just to get paid? Clumsy. We figured who you are on Zenti shouldn’t live or die with a SIM card.

How it works

So you get a Zen Tag — a clean handle like @wanjiku. Share that instead of your number. Anyone can search your name or tag, find you, and pay or request in one tap. It’s also a real tag you can physically touch a phone to, so the tag is how Zenti knows it’s really you. Simple, na ni yako.

For creators & groups

And if you build an audience, your tag can double as your promo code. Team up with brands or your community, share your handle as the code, and earn from whatever it pulls in. One handle for your people, your payments and your partnerships — no separate links to keep track of.

✓ One handle. Findable, payable, and fully yours — even if your line isn’t.

03 — Quick pay

Paid in under
five seconds.

Where it came from

At a busy till or a roadside stall, the slow part was never the money — it was the menus. Open the app, wait, find the right screen, tap through, tap again. Meanwhile a queue is forming behind you. We thought paying should be the quickest thing you touch all day, not the most fiddly.

How it works

So we built Quick Access. The moment you open Zenti, it drops you straight onto the Pay screen — no digging. Type the amount, double-tap to confirm with Face ID, and you’re done. Open, amount, confirm. Chap chap.

✓ The fastest screen you’ll touch all day — done before you’ve put the phone down.

04 — Money links

Send a link.
Get paid back.

Where it came from

You know the dance. “Send me your number… okay sending now… did you get it? Lemme see the screenshot.” That little back-and-forth every time a friend owes you is a quiet daily tax on everyone. It’s small, but it adds up, and it’s annoying.

How it works

So instead, you make a link for any amount and drop it in WhatsApp or a text. They tap it, pay with M-Pesa or their balance, and you get the ping the moment the pesa lands — even if they’ve never opened Zenti in their life. No app required on their side.

✓ No numbers handed out, no screenshots chased. Just a link, then paid.

05 — Split the bill

Split it.
Skip the maths.

Where it came from

One person always ends up covering the table, the trip, or the group gift — and then spends the next week doing maths in their head and texting everyone “bro, my share?” It’s awkward, it sours the mood, and honestly, money between friends shouldn’t strain the friendship.

How it works

So we let you split any amount across the group in a tap — evenly, or with custom shares if someone had the extra nyama choma. Everyone gets a request on their Zen Tag, pays their bit, and you watch who’s settled update live. No spreadsheet, no group-chat nagging.

✓ Everyone pays their share — and you keep the friendship intact.

06 — Tap to pay

Two phones touch.
It’s done.

Where it came from

At a stall, or just paying back a friend, the slowest bit was never the amount — you know what you owe. It’s the “who.” Finding the right merchant, typing in a till namba without fumbling. We thought: si lazima. If the person is standing right there, the phone should just know.

How it works

So you enter the amount and hold your phone up to a Zen Tag or another phone. NFC picks up exactly who you’re paying in an instant, you confirm with Face ID or your fingerprint, and it’s sent. Input, tap, pay. That’s the whole thing.

✓ No till to type. No paying the wrong person by mistake.

07 — Scan & go

Point, confirm,
walk on.

Where it came from

Queues build up for one reason: paying takes too long. Everyone’s waiting while one person hunts through menus. A scan should be the quickest moment of your day, not the part that holds up the whole line.

How it works

So you open the camera, point it at any Zenti QR, check the amount is right, and confirm. The receipt pops up on both screens at the same time, so there’s no “did it go through?” moment. Setting up a merchant QR is a one-time KES 100 — small, but we’d rather tell you straight than hide it.

✓ Paid and walking off before the cashier even looks up.

08 — Savings goals

Saving, without
thinking about it.

Where it came from

Almost everyone means to save. But the money quietly leaks out through everyday spending — a little here, a little there — and by month-end there’s nothing left to put aside. We learned that willpower alone almost never fills a goal. The trick is to make saving happen without you having to decide every time.

How it works

So you set a target — a trip, a new phone, a cushion for when things go sideways. Switch on round-ups, and every payment you make spares a few bob to top it up. Ndogo ndogo, it grows. You just watch the ring fill, and you can pull your money out any time you want.

✓ Real progress you can see, building quietly on its own.

09 — Pools

Save together,
see everything.

Where it came from

Chamas, group trips, shared rent — pooling money has always meant one person holding everyone’s cash, a notebook somewhere, and a whole lot of trust. And we all know how that goes: a record goes missing, someone swears they paid, and suddenly the goodwill is gone too. We wanted the money and the trust to live in the same place.

How it works

So you create a pool, invite your people, and everyone sees the same thing — who paid, how much, and what’s left — updating live. Contributions, the running total, and any withdrawals all sit on one screen for the whole group to see. Hakuna kucheza na pesa ya watu.

✓ Group money everyone can actually trust, because everyone can see it.

10 — Zen Card

A card that’s
actually yours.

Where it came from

Most cards feel like they belong to the bank, not to you. You can’t control them properly, the perks are buried where you never see them, and freezing one means calling a hotline and waiting. We wanted to flip that — a card that answers to you, instantly.

How it works

So you can freeze and unfreeze it in a single tap, see your spending sorted neatly by category, and earn perks at the places you actually shop. Drop your transit pass, ID and tickets into the same wallet too. It’s wired straight to your Zenti balance, so what you see is what you’ve got.

✓ Full control, right in your pocket. Sorted. See the Zen Card →
