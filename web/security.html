Security & policies

Your money,
guarded carefully.

Security is not a feature we add at the end. It shapes how Zenti is built, from the moment you open the app to the moment your money lands. Here is how we protect your account and your data, in plain words.

Your money isn't tied to your SIM. With M-Pesa and most mobile money, your money lives on your phone line. If your SIM is stolen, swapped, lost, or your phone is damaged, you can be locked out, and SIM-swap fraud is a real worry here. Zenti is built differently. You do not need an active phone line to transact. Your account lives on the Zenti network behind your identity, your device, and your biometrics, not your SIM card. Lose the line or swap the phone and your money is still yours and still safe. Sign in on a new device, verify it is you, and you are back.

The short version: your money is locked behind your face or fingerprint, your data is encrypted, your account does not depend on your SIM, and you can freeze it in seconds. We never sell your personal information.

Encrypted, end to end

Your data is encrypted while it travels to us and while it sits in our systems. Even if someone intercepted it, they would see scrambled noise, not your balance.

Your face or fingerprint

Payments are confirmed with Face ID or your fingerprint on your own device. No one can move your money by guessing a PIN over your shoulder.

We confirm it is really you

Like every regulated money service in Kenya, we verify your identity (KYC) when you open an account. This keeps fraudsters out and your account yours.

You stay in control

Freeze your card in one tap, see every transaction the moment it happens, and get a clear alert for anything that looks off. Nothing happens quietly.

We collect less, never sell

We only ask for the information we genuinely need to run your account and meet the law. We do not sell your personal data to anyone, full stop.

A clear way to get help

If something goes wrong or a payment looks wrong, there is a real path to flag it and have it looked into. You should never feel stuck.

Not tied to your SIM

Your account lives on the Zenti network behind your identity and biometrics, not your phone line. A lost, swapped, or stolen SIM does not lock you out of your money.

New devices get verified

A new phone cannot move your money until you prove it is you. You can see every device on your account and sign out any you do not recognise.

Confirms in real time

Tap to pay reads a Zen Tag, so money goes to the right person, not a mistyped number. No more sending cash to a stranger by one wrong digit.

How we protect your account

Good security works in layers, so that no single mistake can hand over your money. Here is what those layers look like at Zenti.

  • Encryption everywhere. Information is encrypted in transit (as it moves between your phone and us) and at rest (while it is stored). This is the baseline, and we hold ourselves to it.
  • Device-level confirmation. Payments and sensitive changes need Face ID, a fingerprint, or your passcode on your own device. The keys stay on your phone, not floating around the internet.
  • Alerts you can trust. You get a notification for every transaction. If you see something you did not do, you can freeze your account immediately and tell us.
  • Freeze in seconds. Misplaced your phone or just being cautious? Freeze your card from the app and unfreeze it the same way once you are sure.
  • Access kept tight. Inside Zenti, access to systems and data is limited to the people who genuinely need it to do their job, and activity is logged.

Not tied to your SIM card

On M-Pesa and most mobile money in Kenya, your money is tethered to your phone line. That is exactly what SIM-swap fraudsters target: take over your number and they can try to take over your money. A lost SIM, a damaged phone, or a line you forgot to keep active can leave you locked out at the worst moment.

Zenti is designed so your account does not depend on your SIM. You do not need an active phone line to transact. Your account sits on the Zenti network behind three things you control:

  • Your identity, verified when you opened the account.
  • Your device, which has to be recognised before it can move money.
  • Your biometrics, your face or fingerprint, to confirm it is really you.

Swap your phone or lose your line and your money is still yours. Sign in on a new device, verify it is you, and you are back where you left off.

Confirming a payment

Paying should be quick, but never accidental. Every payment is authorised by you, on your device, in the moment.

  • You confirm every payment. Face ID, your fingerprint, or your passcode signs off each one. Nothing leaves your account without that.
  • Quick confirm, on purpose. The pay screen uses a short double-tap to send, so paying is fast but takes a deliberate action. A stray touch in your pocket does not pay anyone.
  • New devices must be verified. A phone you have not used before cannot move money until you prove it is you on it.
  • See and remove devices. You can view every device signed in to your account and sign out any you do not recognise, in seconds.

Safe by design when you pay

Sending money should not feel like a gamble. These are the choices that make a Zenti payment feel certain.

  • Confirms in real time. A payment clears the moment you confirm it, so you avoid the familiar "money gone, order not updated" limbo. Both sides see it land.
  • The right recipient. Tap to pay reads a Zen Tag to identify who you are paying, so a single mistyped digit cannot send your money to a stranger.
  • Freeze instantly. Freeze your card or your whole account from the app the moment something feels wrong, and unfreeze it the same way when you are sure.
  • Every payment is recorded. Each transaction is logged and searchable, so you can always find what you paid, when, and to whom.

Your data & privacy

Your personal information belongs to you. We handle it in line with the Kenya Data Protection Act 2019, and we try to keep our promises simple enough that you can actually read them.

What we collect, and why

We collect what we need to give you a working, lawful money account, and not more:

  • Who you are (your name, ID details, phone number, and similar) so we can verify your identity and meet KYC rules.
  • How you use Zenti (your transactions and account activity) so we can show your money clearly and spot fraud.
  • Device and security signals so we can keep your account safe and confirm it is really you signing in.

Your rights

Under the Data Protection Act 2019, you have real rights over your information, and we will help you use them:

  • See it. Ask for a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Fix it. Ask us to correct anything that is wrong or out of date.
  • Delete it. Ask us to delete your data, within the limits the law allows (some records we are required to keep for a set period).
  • Object. Tell us how you do and do not want your information used.

To make any of these requests, email privacy@zentipay.app and we will guide you through it.

Regulatory standards

Zenti is early and currently in testing. We want to be honest about that rather than dress it up.

We are building Zenti to meet the standards expected of money services in Kenya, including the expectations of the Central Bank of Kenya (CBK) and the requirements of the Kenya Data Protection Act 2019. As we grow, our aim is to operate within the right regulatory framework for the services we offer, and to work with licensed and regulated partners where the rules call for it.

We will keep this page updated as our status changes, so what you read here always reflects where we actually stand.

We will never ask for your full PIN, password, or one-time code by phone, SMS, or email. If anyone does, it is not us. Hang up and report it.

Reporting a problem

If you spot a security issue, a suspicious transaction, or a possible vulnerability, please tell us so we can act on it quickly.

  • Security and fraud: security@zentipay.app
  • Privacy and data requests: privacy@zentipay.app
  • Anything else: hello@zentipay.app

If you are a security researcher, we appreciate responsible disclosure. Send us the details and give us a fair chance to fix the issue before sharing it publicly.
